What is this. game?
Osu! Tatakae! Ouendan is a rhythm game for the Nintendo DS made by iNiS (known for another good rhythm game, Gitaroo Man). Quietly released in Japan on July of 2005, this game experienced poor sales, and it looked like the end of the line. However, rumors of its awesomeness drifted to the United States, and suddenly it became a hot import game. So hot, in fact, that iNiS graced us with its American brother, the Elite Beat Agents, not to mention that the sequel was released not too long ago.
But what do you do in this?
The game's focus is the use of the touch screen to make your stylus tap, slide, and spin like a crack monkey to the beat of a song. One of my friends described it as 'Dance Dance Revolution for people who don't want to exercise.' Even though it's pretty much in another language, the gameplay is really easy to pick up, especially to those familiar to EBA.
Another language? You mean that squiggly writing that I saw in that really strange porno the other day?

Whoa, you know Japanese?! I didn't know that!

Ummm. so what is the plot anyway?
Each stage has a character who's completely in a pickle. I'm talking about a really, really crappy situation that just makes them lose it and shout 'OUENDAAAAAN!' (HEEEAAALP!) Suddenly, the Cheer Squad rushes in and motivates the character through the tough times using the powers of inspirational dancing!
